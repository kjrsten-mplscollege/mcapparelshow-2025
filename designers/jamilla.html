Jamilla Anisa Bint-Charles Bryant

Fervent Memories

Model Phoebe Josephine poses casually, swathed head to toe in comfy knits. The pants are made from a stretchy nylon Lycra with a yellow gingham print and are fitted from hip to knee, ending in two gathered tiers.

The back of the cardigan features a kite-shaped cutout formed by the intersection of the inverted V of the yoke and the deep cowl below. The cowl is accented by knife pleats and frames the baby-inspired print that decorates the tank top underneath.

These comfortable garments made of stretchy Lycra blends in a range of soft colors give the wearer a wide range of motion. Model Phoebe Josephine’s dynamic pose brings the garments to life as they follow her unconfined movements.

Designer Bio

Jamilla was born and raised in North Minneapolis. Her African American Muslim background is integral to her designs, influencing her choices in cut, fit, and color. Early in her life, she was fascinated with styling and accessorizing, which eventually led her to becoming her family’s go-to stylist. But soon, styling wasn't enough to satisfy her creative spirit, so she sought out Minneapolis College’s Apparel Design program to master the art of clothing alteration and construction. Her focus is to create memorable looks for members of the film and music industry, but she also hopes to explore the many possibilities the fashion world has to offer.

Designer Statement

My collection “Fervent Memories,” is inspired by the clothing I dreamed of owning as I was growing up. I have always lived with a clear vision of my style, but as a hijabi I often struggled to find the perfect garments to fit my stylistic needs. Each look represents a phase of my life up to the present day, using layered interchangeable garments that speak with their own voice, but collectively say, “I am here with no fear.” I made this the focus of my collection to bring closure to this period of my life and move forward to the next dream. This collection is a visual embodiment of my journey towards mastering comfortability, movement, modesty, and swag. With this collection, I hope to create a middle ground in current women’s fashion, walking the line between modest and revealing clothing and filling the empty space between these two extremes.
