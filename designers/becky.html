Designer Bio

Becky grew up in a very small town in south-eastern Minnesota. She has a bachelor’s degree in theatre from the University of Minnesota and has worked in theatre in various capacities for over twenty years. Proving that it’s never too late to learn a new skill, she didn’t start sewing until she was in her late thirties and is now pursuing work in costuming. As a devotee of fashion and costume history, she also writes about fashion and film for Perisphere: The Trylon Cinema’s Blog.

Designer Statement

My collection, “Côte d'Azur,” is inspired by films set along the French Riviera in the 1950s. I love how fashionably dressed people appeared in their everyday lives back then, and the silhouettes of that era have been a constant source of inspiration for me. The New Look of the tailored bodice and full skirt debuted by Christian Dior in 1947 continued to influence fashion well into the 1960s. The florals in my collection are drawn from the vivid colors of the French flower market captured on screen in Hitchcock’s To Catch a Thief, while the stripes are reminiscent of classic Brenton tops frequently donned by Jean Seberg. Dressing up shouldn’t be saved for special occasions, and I hope this collection inspires people to dress up just because they want to.
