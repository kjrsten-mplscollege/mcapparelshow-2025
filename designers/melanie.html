Melanie Lee

Zoo Nkauj Design

Model Alena Lee poses confidently wearing a matched set of top and skirt perfect for the Hmong New Year. Inspired by the fabrics used in traditional Thai clothing, the set is made of a red floral jacquard.

The strapless top is inspired by the pleated sabai, a traditional Thai garment, and features a pleated fan above the ruching at center front. The skirt is made of the same material as the top above a tier of gathered chiffon.

The look is accented by a matching set of traditional Hmong accessories, a silver belt and necklace. The necklace is a large silver collar with dangling strings of medallions that drape down the model’s chest and back.

Designer Bio

Melanie was born in north Minneapolis. Melanie loves to sketch wedding gowns and hopes to become a designer for bridal and special occasion wear. Growing up, her mother collected traditional Hmong clothing to give to her daughters as a wedding gift, which inspired her to design her own garments.

Designer Statement

My collection “Zoo Nkauj Design,” which means beautiful designs in Hmong, is inspired by traditional Hmong clothing. The garments are paired with traditional Hmong accessories to complete the looks. With this collection, I hope to create garments that blend modern and traditional styles.
